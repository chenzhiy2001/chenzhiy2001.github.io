Step 9: Add visual elements and formatting to increase the map’s effectiveness.

Although visual maps are about productivity, not looks, additional visuals and formatting can call attention to specific concepts and add visual interest.

  • Colors: Your brain thrives on color and imagery, so take the time to add color to the concepts, lines, and text of your visual map.
  • Icons: Icons add meaning and context to visual maps, making it easier to visually classify a map’s contents. If you use them in your maps, be sure to create a legend to explain what they mean. Visual mapping programs usually have a library of icons available for use.
  • Images: This is another way to add visual appeal to your maps. Images help enhance the meaning and context of your map and help others to interpret the map’s contents. Don’t add random images for decoration. Most visual mapping software programs include extensive image libraries.
