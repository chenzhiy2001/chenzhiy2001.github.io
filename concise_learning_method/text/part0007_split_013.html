Step 10: Go through the visual map checklist for any final edits.

After the preliminary visual map is constructed, it should be revised as many times as needed to remain up-to-date and meaningful.

The final visual map should adhere to the following guidelines:

  • Map is well organized and clear.
  • Content is factually accurate and complete.
  • Concepts are logically arranged and follow a natural progression.
  • Concepts are succinctly presented with minimal text.
  • Visual map shows what you have learned about the topic and what you want presented.
  • Links show correct and meaningful relationships between each connected concept.
  • Labels accurately describe the relationship between concepts.
  • Text is clear and easy to read.
  • Color is effectively used for emphasis and increased comprehension.
  • Graphics are used only when necessary to increase comprehension.
  • If someone other than you will be using the map, it makes sense to the intended audience.
  • Spelling and grammar are correct throughout the map.
